HONEYPOT
The purpose of a honeypot is to refine an organization's intrusion detection system (IDS) and threat response
so it is in a better position to manage and prevent attacks. There are two primary kinds of honeypots: production and research Improve intrusion detection and threat response Honeypots help organizations refine their intrusion detection systems and threat response to better manage and prevent attacks. Gather information about attackers Honeypots can help organizations learn about attacker tactics, techniques, and procedures. This information can help organizations identify potential vulnerabilities in their systems and prioritize security efforts. Reduce risk of false positives Honeypots are less likely to attract legitimate activity than traditional cybersecurity measures. There are two main types of honeypots: Production honeypots Used by large organizations as an active defense to identify compromises in their internal networks. Research honeypots Used by governments or large cybersecurity organizations to track the development of advanced persistent threats. Honeypots can be applied to any computing resource, including software, networks, file servers, and routers..

FEATURES OF HONEYPOT
A pure honeypot refers to a full-scale system running on various servers. It completely mimics the production system. Within a pure honeypot is data made to look confidential, as well as “sensitive” user information, which have a number of sensors used to track and observe attacker activity.
